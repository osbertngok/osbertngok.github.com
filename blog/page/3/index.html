From Dependence to Independence
03-18-2010
thoughts

The recent experience in UPenn helps me understand why it is important to put independence before interdependence. Think about this scenario: suppose you want to invest in interdependent relationship. If succeeds, you gain a lot more than you can do when you are alone. If fails, the loss depends on what type of person you are. If you are dependent, you lose everything by the definition of “dependence”: you can only rely on one thing at one time. This is not true in general, but it is possibly true for a dependent person. He or she trusts his / her parents or loved one without preconditions, but the choices are mutual exclusive. If you are independent, you move backward to your original state “independence”, which is still acceptable. This is not so bad so the investment is profitable.
